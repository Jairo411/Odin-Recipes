Reciept To Make Los Pollos Hermanos Fried Chicken

Description

If you've been living under a rock for the past 10 years, you probably have heard of the critically acclaimed show breaking bad. In this tv show walter white and jessie pinkman come into contact with a big time drug dealer gustavo fring. Gustavo fring, has multiple money laundering business. One of those business is his fried chicken franchise los pollos hermanos. Instead of writing down a recipe for creating 99.9% crystal blue meth. Here is a recipe for creating that beautiful fried chicken seen in breaking bad in those los pollos hermanos scenes.

Ingredients

Chicken & Spice Brine Ingredients:

  • 15 g cayenne pepper
  • 10 g aleppo Pepper
  • 10 g ground coriander
  • 15 g red pepper falkes, ground
  • 15 g kosher salt (add for dry brine)
  • 1 whole Chicken
  • Frying oil
  • Flour

Chicken Batter Ingredients:

  • 100 g all-purpose Flour
  • 100 g cornstarch
  • Sprinkle spice mixture
  • 250 g water
  • 30 g vodka
  • 2 large eggs

Fries & Batter Ingredients

  • 2 russet potatoes, spiralized
  • Boiling water
  • 15 g paprika
  • 60 g all-purpose flour
  • 15 g cayenne pepper
  • 6 g onion powder
  • Kosher salt
  • 3 Tbsp spice mixture
  • 150 g water
  • 1 quart peanut oil

Method

Chicken Method:

  1. Into a small bowl, combine 15 grams of cayenne pepper, 10 grams each of
    Aleppo pepper and ground coriander, and 15 grams of red pepper flakes that
    have been ground in a spice grinder. Wisk to combine.

  2. Set aside 3 tablespoons for the curly fries. Turn the rest into a dry brine for
    curly fries. Turn the rest into a dry brine for the chicken by adding 15 grams of kosher salt.
    Whisk to combine.

  3. Using 1 whole chicken, cut into the classic 10 piece fried chicken pieces.

  4. Load the pieces onto a wire rack set in a rimmed baking sheet and generously sprikle
    on all sides with the dry-brine mixture. Place in thefridge uncovered for 4-24 hours.

  5. For the batter, in a medium bowl combien 100 grams each of all-purpose flour and cornstarch,
    an optional sprikle of the spice mixture, 250 grams of water, 30 grams of vodka, and 2 large eggs.
    Whisk together until a pancake batter consistency is reached. Cover and refrigerate for a couple of hours.

  6. Using the batter, along with a pie pan of flour, dunk each piece of chicken into the floru followed by
    the batter making sure each piece is throughly coated. Rinse and repeat for all chicken pieces.

  7. In a 375°F pot of frying oil, carefully place the chicken pieces into the oil(facing away from you, no splashes!)
    and frying 7-10 minutes depending on the size of the piece. Turn every other minute or so to make sure no scorching is
    developing. Once golden brown and 175-185°F for dark meat, 165F for white meat, remove.

  8. Drain and cool on a paper towel-lined baking sheet before serving.

Curly Fries Method

  1. Using a spiralizer, lock in 1 russet potato, and voer a bowl of boiling water begin spiralizing the potato,
    and over a bowl of boiling water begin spiralizing the potato. Rinse and repeat with remaining potatoes.
    Make sure all the potato twists are underwater.

  2. Let sit for 15 mintues then drain.

  3. Once drained, dump the potatoes onto a paper towel-lined baking tray and sandwich between paper towels and
    pat until as dry as possible.

  4. After the potatoes are fully dry, grab some scissors and cut the potatoes into shorter pieces. Not too long, but not too
    short due to curling up in the frying oil.

  5. Once the potatoes have been snipped, place back onto the baking sheet covering with a paper towel while the batter is made.

  6. Into a large bowl, combine 15 grams of paprika, 60 grams of all-purpose flour, 15 grams of cayenee pepper, 6 grams of onion
    power, and a tiny pinch of kosher salt. Add in the reserved spiec blend and whisk to combine.

  7. Add in 150 grams of water (or however much is required) and whisk together until a little lighter than the consistency of paint.

  8. Once the right consistency is reached, dump in the potatoes and give them a toss until thoroughly coated and battered.

  9. In a cast-iron skillet filled with 1 quart of peanut oil heated to 350°F, carefully and gently lower the fries into the
    oil in batches and spread out evenly and fry for 2-5 minutes until a deep reddish-brown.

  10. Remove and drain on a paper towel-lined baking sheet and generously season with kosher salt while still warm.
